# channels

## Definition
The `channels` tag identifies content about mechanisms for reaching, acquiring, and distributing value to customers - how enterprises connect with their customer types to deliver solutions.

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to content that:
- Defines channel strategy development approaches
- Discusses customer acquisition and distribution methods
- Provides frameworks for channel selection and optimization
- Analyzes channel effectiveness across different contexts

## Always Include When
- Content focuses on customer acquisition strategies
- Channel selection and optimization frameworks
- Distribution and delivery mechanism design
- Multi-channel strategy development

## Never Include When
- General marketing content without strategic channel focus
- Distribution logistics without strategic channel focus

## Required Combinations
- Must include appropriate canvas-sections hierarchy: [economic-model, channels]

## Content Examples
- Channel strategy frameworks
- Customer acquisition channel analysis
- Distribution channel design methodologies
- Channel effectiveness measurement approaches

## Related Tags
- Parent tags: [economic-model](../economic-model.md)
- Often appears with customers (channels reach customers)
- Often paired with [revenue](../revenue/revenue.md) (channels enable revenue)
- May combine with venture-stage tags for stage-appropriate channels

## Key Concepts
- Direct vs. indirect channels
- Digital vs. physical distribution
- Channel partner relationships
- Customer acquisition costs
- Channel scalability
- Multi-sided platform dynamics
