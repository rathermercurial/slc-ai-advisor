# product-market-fit

## Definition
The `product-market-fit` tag identifies content about achieving the point where a product satisfies strong market demand at scale.

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to content that:
- Defines product-market fit indicators and metrics
- Discusses techniques for achieving and measuring PMF
- Provides frameworks for PMF validation
- Analyzes product-market fit in case studies

## Always Include When
- Content focuses on PMF indicators and measurement
- Market validation at scale
- Growth and retention metrics indicating fit
- Transition from early-stage to growth-stage

## Never Include When
- Earlier stage validation (use job-customer-fit or job-solution-fit)
- General growth strategies without PMF focus
- Pre-validation product development

## Required Combinations
- Often combined with venture-stage tags (early-stage, growth-stage)
- May include canvas-sections: [customer-model, key-metrics]

## Content Examples
- Product-market fit measurement frameworks
- PMF indicators and signals
- Scaling after achieving PMF

## Related Tags

- Related to [key-metrics](canvas/key-metrics.md)
- Related to [channels](canvas/channels.md) for scaling

- Sean Ellis 40% test
- Retention and engagement metrics
- Organic growth signals
- Customer pull vs. push
