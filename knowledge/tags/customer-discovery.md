# customer-discovery

## Definition
The `customer-discovery` tag identifies content about the systematic process of validating customer assumptions through direct engagement, interviews, and research.

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to content that:
- Defines customer discovery methodologies
- Discusses interview and research techniques
- Provides guidance on validating customer assumptions
- Analyzes discovery processes in case studies

## Always Include When
- Content focuses on customer research methodologies
- Customer interview frameworks and techniques
- Assumption testing with customers
- Discovery phase case studies

## Never Include When
- General customer analysis not focused on discovery
- Post-validation customer segmentation
- Customer acquisition strategies

## Required Combinations
- Often combined with venture-stage tags (especially idea-stage, early-stage)
- May include canvas-sections: [customer-model]

## Content Examples
- Customer interview guides
- Discovery research frameworks
- Assumption validation techniques
- Mom Test principles

## Related Tags
- Related to [customers](canvas/customers.md)
- Often appears with [jobs-to-be-done](canvas/jobs-to-be-done.md)
- May combine with [early-adopters](early-adopters.md)

## Key Concepts
- Problem-solution fit validation
- Customer interview techniques
- Assumption identification and testing
- Pivoting based on discovery insights
