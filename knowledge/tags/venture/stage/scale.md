# scale-stage

## Definition
The `scale-stage` tag identifies content specifically relevant to scale-stage social enterprises focused on achieving significant impact, influencing systems, and ensuring long-term sustainability.

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to content that:
- Addresses challenges specific to scale-stage ventures
- Provides guidance for systems-level impact
- Discusses sustainability and institutionalization approaches
- Analyzes scale-stage case studies and examples

## Always Include When
- Content specifically targets scale-stage venture needs
- Systems change and influence frameworks
- Long-term sustainability approaches
- Scale-stage impact and legacy strategies

## Never Include When
- Content applies equally to all venture stages
- Content is specific to other stages (idea, early, growth)
- General scaling content not about systems-level change

## Required Combinations
- Must include `venture-stage-model`

## Content Examples
- Systems change frameworks
- Sustainability and institutionalization strategies
- Scale-stage impact measurement approaches
- Legacy and influence methodologies

## Related Tags
- Parent tag: `venture-stage-model`
- Often appears with `impact` (achieving systems-level impact)
- Often paired with `advantage` (sustainable competitive advantages)
- May combine with `strategy-model` (sophisticated strategic approaches)

## Stage Characteristics
- Achieving significant systems-level impact
- Influencing policy and institutional change
- Ensuring long-term organizational sustainability
- Building lasting competitive advantages
- Creating industry or sector transformation
- Developing sophisticated strategic approaches
- Establishing organizational legacy
