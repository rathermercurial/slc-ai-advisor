# economic-model

## Definition
The `economic-model` tag identifies content about how customer value creation translates into financially sustainable operations, ensuring the enterprise can maintain and scale its impact through viable economics.

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to content that:
- Explains economic model frameworks and sustainability approaches
- Discusses business model economics and financial viability
- Provides guidance on economic model development
- Analyzes economic model effectiveness across ventures

## Always Include When
- Content covers multiple economic model components
- Economic sustainability frameworks and methodologies
- Business model economics analysis
- Financial viability assessment approaches

## Never Include When
- Content focuses on only one specific sub-component (use specific tag instead)
- General finance content not tied to canvas framework
- Accounting or financial management not related to business model economics

## Required Combinations
- Must include appropriate canvas-sections hierarchy: [economic-model]
- Often combined with specific sub-component tags when content spans multiple areas

## Content Examples
- Economic model development frameworks
- Financial sustainability methodologies
- Business model economics analysis
- Economic model evaluation approaches

## Related Tags
- Sub-component tags: [channels](channels/channels.md), [revenue](revenue/revenue.md), [costs](costs/costs.md), [advantage](advantage/advantage.md), [financial-model](financial-model/financial-model.md)
- Often combined with `concept` content tags for economic theories
- May appear with venture-stage tags for stage-specific economic approaches

- [Channels](channels/channels.md) - Customer acquisition and distribution mechanisms
