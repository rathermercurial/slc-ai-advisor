# concept

## Definition
The `concept` tag identifies content that explains fundamental concepts, theoretical frameworks, and intellectual foundations that underpin the Social Lean Canvas methodology.

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to content that:
- Explains theoretical foundations and concepts
- Provides intellectual background for framework elements
- Discusses conceptual relationships and principles
- Explores academic or theoretical perspectives

## Always Include When
- Content focuses on conceptual understanding
- Theoretical background material
- Foundational concept explanations
- Academic or research-based content supporting the framework

## Never Include When
- Content is purely practical or instructional
- Content contains examples without conceptual depth
- Administrative or procedural content

## Required Combinations
- Often combined with canvas section tags when explaining section concepts
- May include `lexicon-entry` for definitional concept content

## Content Examples
- Leverage and network effects concepts
- Innovation frameworks and theories
- Social entrepreneurship theoretical foundations
- Business model concept explanations

## Related Tags
- Often appears with specific canvas section tags for section concepts
- May combine with `lexicon-entry` for conceptual definitions
- Often paired with `strategy-model` for strategic concepts

## Sub-Categories
- `leverage` - Strategic leverage concepts
- `innovation` - Innovation frameworks and approaches
- Additional concept sub-categories as they develop

## Content Characteristics
- Theoretical depth and rigor
- Conceptual frameworks and models
- Academic or research foundation
- Intellectual background for practical application
- Cross-disciplinary insights
