# product-sales

## Definition
The `product-sales` tag identifies ventures that generate revenue primarily through selling tangible goods directly to customers, whether B2C or B2B.

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to ventures that:
- Manufacture and sell physical products
- Distribute tangible goods to end customers
- Generate primary revenue from product transactions
- Focus on product-based business models
- Include both direct sales and retail distribution

## Always Include When
- Manufacturing ventures selling their products
- Product-based social enterprises
- Retail operations with product focus
- Consumer goods with social/environmental mission
- Hardware or physical technology products

## Never Include When
- Service-based revenue models
- Digital products or software (unless packaged as physical goods)
- Subscription models (use `subscription` instead)
- Licensing of products (use `licensing-royalty` instead)

## Required Combinations
- Must include `venture-type` and `revenue-source`
- Often combined with manufacturing or product-focused industry tags
- May combine with specific impact mechanisms

## Content Examples
- Solar lanterns and clean energy products
- Sustainable apparel and fashion items
- Health and nutrition products
- Educational materials and tools
- Eco-friendly consumer goods
- Agricultural products and equipment

## Related Tags
- Parent tags: `venture-type`, `revenue-source`
- Industry alignment: `manufacturing`, `apparel`, `clean-energy`, `agriculture`
- Impact mechanisms: `product-service-impact`
- Often paired with: `impact-equity`, `bootstrapped`

## Key Concepts
- Tangible goods and products
- Direct customer sales
- Product development and manufacturing
- Distribution and retail channels
- Product-market fit
- Scalable production models

## Revenue Characteristics
- Transaction-based revenue
- One-time or repeat purchases
- Potential for economies of scale
- Inventory and supply chain considerations
- Physical distribution requirements
- Product development investment needs
