# participants

## Definition
The `participants` tag identifies content about key stakeholders directly involved or impacted by the enterprise's activities in the impact model (may overlap with customers but includes broader stakeholder ecosystem).

## Usage Criteria
Apply this tag to content that:
- Defines stakeholder identification and mapping approaches
- Discusses participant engagement strategies
- Provides frameworks for stakeholder analysis
- Analyzes participant roles and relationships

## Always Include When
- Content focuses on impact stakeholder identification
- Stakeholder mapping and analysis frameworks
- Participant engagement strategies
- Stakeholder relationship management

## Never Include When
- Customer analysis content (use `customers` unless overlap with impact participants)
- General stakeholder management not tied to impact model
- Organizational stakeholders not related to impact activities

## Required Combinations
- Must include appropriate canvas-sections hierarchy: [impact-model, participants]

## Content Examples
- Stakeholder mapping frameworks
- Participant identification methodologies
- Stakeholder engagement strategies
- Participant analysis templates

## Related Tags
- Parent tags: [impact-model](../impact-model.md)
- Often appears with [issue](../issue/issue.md) (participants affected by issue)
- Often paired with [activities](../activities/activities.md) (participants involved in activities)
- May combine with customers when overlap exists

## Key Concepts
- Direct vs. indirect participants
- Beneficiaries vs. contributors
- Stakeholder influence and interest
- Participant engagement levels
- Multi-stakeholder ecosystems
- Participant-customer overlap
- Community and beneficiary involvement
