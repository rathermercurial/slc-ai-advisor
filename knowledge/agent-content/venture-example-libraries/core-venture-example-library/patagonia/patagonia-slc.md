# Patagonia - Social Lean Canvas

## Canvas Overview
---
**Venture:** Patagonia
**Date:** 2024-01-15
**Version:** 1.0
---

## Canvas Structure

### Purpose
We're in business to save our home planet by combating the environmental crisis.

### Impact
A thriving planet with stabilized climate and intact ecosystems for future generations.
*[See full Impact Model](patagonia-impact-model.md)*

### Customer Model

#### Customers
Outdoor enthusiasts and environmentally conscious consumers who value quality gear aligned with their ethics.

**Early Adopters:**
Hardcore climbers and surfers in the 1970s who prized durable equipment that wouldn't harm nature.

#### Jobs to be Done
Customers need rugged outdoor gear that performs in harsh conditions while minimizing environmental guilt.

**Existing Alternatives:**
Other outdoor brands like The North Face and Arc'teryx, or cheap disposable gear with unknown ethics.

#### Unique Value Proposition
High-performance, lifetime-wear outdoor gear backed by a company that puts the planet first.

#### Solution
Sustainable outdoor clothing made from recycled materials, backed by lifetime repairs and a used gear marketplace.



### Economic Model
#### Channels
Direct sales through 70+ owned stores and e-commerce, with activism serving as primary marketing.

#### Revenue
Premium apparel sales generate $1.5 billion annually from customers willing to pay for sustainability.

#### Costs
Higher costs for sustainable materials, fair labor, repairs, and 1% Earth tax on all sales.


#### Advantage
Decades of authentic environmental commitment creating unmatched brand trust and customer loyalty.


### Key Metrics

$1.5B revenue, 98% recycled materials, $100M annually to climate action, 10% market share.
