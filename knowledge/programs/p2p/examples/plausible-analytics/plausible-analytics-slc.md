## Canvas Structure

### Purpose
Reduce corporate surveillance by providing ethical, privacy-first web analytics that respects user rights while proving sustainable alternatives to surveillance capitalism.

### Impact
Transformed digital analytics from surveillance-based extraction to ethical, privacy-respecting business intelligence demonstrating sustainable alternatives to surveillance capitalism.
*[See full Impact Model](plausible-analytics-impact-model.md)*

-----
### Customer Model

#### Customers
**End Users:** Privacy-conscious website owners and individual creators seeking simple, ethical analytics alternative to surveillance-based tools.
**Buyers:** Decision-makers at SMBs, agencies, and enterprises purchasing analytics tools for organizational use while prioritizing GDPR compliance.
**Resellers:** Agencies and consultants offering white-label Plausible services to clients requiring privacy-first analytics solutions.


**Early Adopters:**
**End Users:** Technical creators and privacy-conscious bloggers who discovered Plausible through Indie Hackers and Hacker News discussions.
**Buyers:** Privacy-conscious SMBs frustrated with GA4 complexity and seeking ethical alternatives for organizational analytics needs.
**Resellers:** Web development agencies valuing transparency and seeking client-friendly dashboards for their privacy-focused client base.



#### Jobs to be Done
**End Users:** Enable ethical business practices, ensure GDPR compliance, and align personal technology choices with privacy values.
**Buyers:** Simplify analytics decision-making, avoid legal complications, and demonstrate organizational commitment to user privacy protection.
**Resellers:** Provide clients with easily shareable, privacy-compliant dashboards while maintaining professional credibility and ethical positioning.



**Existing Alternatives:**
**End Users:** Google Analytics (complex, privacy-invasive), no analytics at all, or basic server logs with limited functionality.
**Buyers:** Google Analytics Universal/GA4, expensive enterprise tools like Adobe Analytics, or manual server log analysis.
**Resellers:** Matomo (complex maintenance), Fathom Analytics (more expensive), or recommending Google Analytics despite privacy concerns.



#### Unique Value Proposition
**End Users:** Simple, privacy-compliant analytics with transparent code enabling values-aligned technology choices without technical complexity.
**Buyers:** GDPR-compliant analytics providing essential insights without consent mechanisms, legal risks, or surveillance capitalism dependency.
**Resellers:** Client-friendly dashboards with white-label options supporting ethical positioning while delivering professional analytics services.



#### Solution
Lightweight, privacy-first web analytics with single-page dashboard, EU hosting, and open-source transparency providing essential metrics without surveillance.

-----
### Economic Model

#### Channels
Content marketing, community engagement, SEO, word-of-mouth referrals, and integration partnerships drive organic growth without paid advertising.

#### Costs
Personnel, EU infrastructure, development, support, and administrative costs maintained lean to enable sustainable growth without external funding.

#### Revenue
Subscription-based SaaS model reaching $3.1M ARR through tiered usage pricing, demonstrating market willingness to pay for privacy-first alternatives.

#### Advantage
Regulatory compliance, technical performance, open source credibility, EU infrastructure, values alignment, bootstrapped independence, and first-mover market positioning.

------
#### Key Metrics
ARR growth, subscriber count, pageviews processed, GitHub engagement, carbon savings, uptime, retention rates, and market share advancement.
