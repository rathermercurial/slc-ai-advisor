# Too Good To Go - Social Lean Canvas

## Canvas Overview
---
**Venture:** Too Good To Go
**Date:** 2025-07-09
**Version:** 1.0
**Source Report:** [Too Good To Go Report](too-good-to-go-report.md)
---

## Canvas Structure

### Purpose
Eliminate food waste worldwide by connecting consumers with surplus food from restaurants and stores, fighting climate change through accessible, collective action.

### Impact
A world without food waste where businesses, consumers, and systems prioritize environmental sustainability, contributing significantly to global climate goals.
*[See full Impact Model](too-good-to-go-impact-model.md)*

-----
### Customer Model

#### Customers
• **Consumers**: Environmentally conscious and budget-minded food buyers seeking discounted surplus food while supporting sustainability goals
• **Businesses**: Restaurants, cafes, bakeries, supermarkets selling surplus food to recover costs and reduce waste

**Early Adopters:**
Environmentally conscious consumers and sustainability-focused food businesses in Denmark seeking solutions to reduce waste and recover costs.

#### Jobs to be Done
• **Consumers**: Access affordable quality food while feeling good about environmental impact and discovering new local food options
• **Businesses**: Convert food waste into revenue while demonstrating sustainability and attracting environmentally conscious customers

**Existing Alternatives:**
• **Consumers**: Discount stores, happy hour deals, timing visits near closing, traditional coupon apps, accepting higher costs for sustainable eating
• **Businesses**: Food banks, manual end-of-day discounts, employee giveaways, composting, waste disposal without revenue recovery

#### Unique Value Proposition
• **Consumers**: Quality food at 50-70% discount with measurable environmental impact, discovery element, and guilt-free consumption aligned with values
• **Businesses**: Transform waste costs into revenue while enhancing brand reputation and attracting environmentally conscious customers

#### Solution
Mobile marketplace connecting businesses with surplus food to consumers through standardized "surprise bags" with integrated payments and environmental impact tracking.

-----
### Economic Model

#### Channels
Digital marketing, app stores, word-of-mouth, media coverage, direct business sales, industry partnerships, and mobile app distribution with physical pickup.

#### Costs
Technology development, marketing, personnel (1,500 employees), impact measurement, educational content, geographic expansion, and operational infrastructure.

#### Revenue
Transaction fees ($1.79 per bag), annual business memberships ($89), enterprise software solutions, and brand partnerships.

#### Advantage
Network effects, first-mover advantage, mission-driven differentiation, operational simplicity, proprietary data assets, policy influence, and authentic ESG positioning.

------
#### Key Metrics
User growth (100M+), business partners (154K+), meals saved (300M+), CO2 prevented (810K tonnes), revenue ($162M), and geographic expansion (19 countries).
